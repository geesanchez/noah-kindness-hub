Note: All stories, photos, and acts of kindness will be shared instantly on our Kindness Inspiration Board . Please follow the guidelines below to help keep our community safe and positive.

Submission Guidelines

1
Appropriate Content

All submissions should be school-appropriate and focus on positive, kind actions. Content should be respectful and suitable for all ages. Avoid sharing personal contact information or full names.

2
Privacy Considerations

Use first names and last initials only. If sharing photos that include other people, ensure you have their permission. For students, parent/guardian permission is required for any identifiable images.

3
Media Quality

Photos and videos should be clear and well-lit. For videos, ensure audio is clear and background noise is minimal. All media should be relevant to kindness, Noah's legacy, or community impact.

4
Instant Posting

All submissions are posted instantly to our Kindness Inspiration Board. Please double-check your post for privacy and appropriateness before submitting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can contribute to Noah's Kindness Hub?

Anyone can contribute! We welcome submissions from students, teachers, staff, parents, and community members. Students under 13 should have parent/guardian assistance when submitting content.

How long will it take for my submission to appear on the site?

Submissions appear instantly on our Kindness Inspiration Board (Padlet). There is no waiting period or review process‚Äîyour act of kindness will be visible right away!

Can I submit something anonymously?

Yes, you can choose to have your submission displayed anonymously on the board. However, please use first names only and do not include personal contact information in your post.

What types of kindness acts should I share?

We welcome all acts of kindness, big or small! This could include helping a classmate, volunteering in the community, creating something to brighten someone's day, or any action that spreads compassion and caring.

How can teachers incorporate Noah's Kindness Hub into their classroom?

Teachers can use the hub as a resource for kindness-themed lessons, encourage students to contribute their own acts of kindness, use the stories as writing prompts, or participate in our monthly kindness challenges as a class activity.

Privacy Notice

Noah's Kindness Hub respects your privacy. We collect minimal personal information (first name, grade level) for submissions. Please do not include full names or personal contact information in your posts.

All content is visible immediately on our Kindness Inspiration Board. Content is archived annually in June, but remains accessible to school administrators.

By submitting content, you grant permission for it to be displayed on Noah's Kindness Hub and potentially used in school-related kindness initiatives.
